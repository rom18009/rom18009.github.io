From Small Town to Big Screen

Preston, Idaho started off with the Bear Lake Massacre in 1863, only a few miles northwest of where the city is currently located. In 1866 Latter-Day Saints started to arrive in the Cache Valley and in 1881 the small town was established. Today the population of Preston is just over 5,500 people. The mixture of agriculture and those looking to get away from the larger cities is what makes up most of the population. In 2004, the movie "Napoleon Dynamite" was filmed in and around locations surrounding Preston and has helped to get the small town some publicity.

In 2014 the cast members of comedy, "Napoleon Dynamite" gathered in Preston for the unveiling of a life size statue of Napoleon Dynamite. The statue is even sporting the "Vote for Pedro" t-shirt and Moon Boots ... more about napolean statue.
